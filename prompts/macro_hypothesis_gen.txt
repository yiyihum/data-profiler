## Instructions
Generate 8-12 hypotheses covering ALL of the following categories (at least 1 per category):

**Category 1: Data Quality & Integrity**
- Missing value patterns (are they random or systematic?)
- Potential data leakage (features that wouldn't be available at prediction time)
- Duplicate or near-duplicate records
- Inconsistent values or encoding issues

**Category 2: Feature Distributions**
- Skewness, multimodality, or heavy tails in numeric features
- Class imbalance in categorical features
- Outlier patterns and their impact

**Category 3: Feature Relationships**
- Multicollinearity between features (redundant features)
- Non-linear relationships between features
- Feature clusters or groups that move together

**Category 4: Text & Structural Patterns** (if text columns exist)
- Text length distributions and their variation across groups
- Vocabulary richness, readability, or structural patterns
- Common phrases, templates, or formulaic content

**Category 5: Temporal Patterns** (if time-related columns exist)
- Trends over time (data drift, distribution shifts)
- Seasonal or cyclical patterns
- Time-based correlations

**Category 6: Target-Feature Relationships**
- Which features show strongest association with the target
- Interaction effects between features on the target
- Subgroup-specific patterns (effects that only appear in certain segments)

IMPORTANT RULES:
- Do NOT focus only on predicting the target. The goal is comprehensive data understanding — data quality, feature engineering opportunities, potential pitfalls, and hidden structures.
- If "Unavailable Fields" are listed in Task Constraints, do NOT generate hypotheses about those fields (they won't be in test data).
- If "Leakage Risk Fields" are listed, generate at least one hypothesis to verify whether they actually leak the target.
- Consider the evaluation metric when thinking about what matters most.
